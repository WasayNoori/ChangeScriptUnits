If you're ever having problems working with over-defined geometry in a sketch, SOLIDWORKS has a tool that helps you automatically resolve any conflicts. It's called the SketchXpert, and it works with both 2D and 3D sketches. Let's take a look by over-defining this sketch. I'll input a conflicting dimension, and leave the dimension driving so that it over-defines the sketch.
 
To activate SketchXpert. simply click on the error message that appears on the bottom of the status bar. This brings up the SketchXpert PropertyManager. The SketchXpert gives you two options to fix the sketch. You can fix it automatically by clicking the diagnose button, or you can use the manual repair mode to see all relations with errors and fix it yourself. Let's look at the automatic option first.
 
I'll start by showing you what happens when you press the Diagnose button. When I do, SketchXpert analyzes the sketch to find solutions to the sketch error. You can see the results box appear in the PropertyManager, and you can use the arrows to toggle through the different possible solutions. Notice in the graphics area, the dimension or relation to be deleted is displayed with a strike through, and an image of the original sketch geometry is still displayed.
 
Once you've found the solution that works best for your design, just click accept, and SketchXpert makes the change for you. A message window shows that the sketch can now find a valid solution, and I can click the green check to exit SketchXpert. But before I do, notice this checkbox here. I can activate this checkbox to automatically launch SketchXpert every time I get an error in a sketch. I'll check it, and exit.
 
Next, let's take a look at the manual option. I'll add another dimension, and again leave it driving to over-define the sketch. Notice SketchXpert launches automatically because of the checkbox I just activated. This time, I'll click Manual Repair. When I do, SOLIDWORKS displays a list of all of the relations and dimensions that have conflicts. As I click through each one, you can see it highlight in the graphics area..., and I can click the Suppressed button to preview how removing it affects the sketch. If you don't like the results, just press undo to return to the full list. Once you've found the dimension or relation you'd like to remove, you can press the delete button. I don't use the delete all button very often, but if you have a sketch that's completely unsolvable and you can't tell what to fix, you can use it to remove every one of the conflicting dimensions and relations. That way, you can start over from scratch. Once everything is fixed the way you want it, just click the green check again to exit the SketchXpert.
